Bots are automated software applications that perform tasks over the internet, ranging from benign activities like web crawling and customer service to malicious attacks such as account takeovers and DDoS1. They can significantly impact internet traffic, skew analytics, and pose security threats to digital businesses. Effective bot management strategies and AI-based solutions are essential to differentiate between good and bad bots, protect against attacks, and maintain operational efficiency. As bots become more sophisticated, mimicking human behavior to evade detection, advanced machine learning algorithms and behavioral analysis are crucial for identifying and mitigating malicious bot activity.

Drones, also known as unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), are small or medium-sized aircraft that can fly remotely and autonomously. They combine robotics with aeronautics and are equipped with modern features like GPS, radar control, infrared sensors, and high- resolution cameras. Drones work by starting the engine, rotating the propellers, and using a remote control or automated GPS navigation. Their stability is maintained by a central flight controller, which adjusts motor revolutions based on real-time data. Drones have various applications, including search and rescue, traffic monitoring, weather measurements, agriculture, military defense, and audiovisual recording. They have transformed industries and opened up new possibilities worldwide.
